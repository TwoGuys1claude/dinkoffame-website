# Social Media Strategy: Driving Traffic to Drill Generator

**Goal**: Get pickleball players to dinkoffame.com/tools/drill-generator via social content
**Core insight**: Players don't search for "drills" — they search for solutions to problems they feel on the court. Start with the pain point, not the drill.

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. "Fix This" Short-Form Videos (Reels / TikTok / Shorts)

Hook with the problem, show the drill, link to the full plan.

**Post ideas:**
- "Your third shot drop keeps hitting the net? Try this 5-minute drill"
- "Can't win at the kitchen line? Here's why (and the drill that fixes it)"
- "3.5 players do THIS wrong — one drill to break the habit"
- "Stop losing to bangers — the reset drill that changes everything"
- "Why you keep popping up your dinks (and how to fix it in 10 minutes)"

**Format:** 15-30 sec. Show the problem -> show the drill -> CTA: "Get your full custom drill plan — link in bio"

---

### 2. "Level Up" Carousel Posts (Instagram / Facebook)

Educational slides that build authority and drive clicks.

**Post ideas:**
- "The 3 drills that took me from 3.0 to 3.5" (1 drill per slide)
- "What to practice at every skill level" (slide per level: 2.5, 3.0, 3.5, 4.0+)
- "Your 15-minute pre-game warm-up routine" (5 slides, 3 drills)
- "Solo drills you can do with just a wall" (4 slides)
- "The practice routine 4.0+ players actually use"

**Last slide CTA:** "Get your custom drill plan at dinkoffame.com — link in bio"

---

### 3. Challenge / Accountability Hooks

Shareable challenges that create repeat engagement.

**Ideas:**
- **7-Day Dink Challenge**: "Can you hit 50 cross-court dinks in a row?" — daily post tracking progress
- **30-Day Drill Streak**: Players share their generated drill plans and check in daily
- **Weekly Drill Drop**: Every Monday, post one drill from the generator with video demo

**Hashtags:** #DinkOfFameDrills #PickleballDrillChallenge #PickleballPractice

---

### 4. Stat-Based Authority Posts

Data-driven posts that build credibility and link naturally to the tool.

**Post ideas:**
- "Players who drill 15 min before playing win 23% more games" (find/cite real data)
- "The #1 skill gap between 3.5 and 4.0 players? Transition game."
- "80% of points in rec play are won on unforced errors — here's what to drill"
- "Average rally length at 3.0 vs 4.0 — and what it means for your practice"

---

## Platform Playbook

### Facebook Groups (highest immediate ROI)
- Pickleball community groups are massive (50k-200k members) and highly engaged
- **Drop value first, not links.** Post a drill tip, let people ask "where can I get more?"
- Share the tool as "free thing I built for the community" — frame as giving back
- Target groups: Pickleball Forum, Pickleball Addicts, local city groups

### Instagram Reels
- Short drill demos perform extremely well in pickleball niche
- Use trending audio + text overlay showing the drill name/focus
- Bio link to dinkoffame.com/tools/drill-generator
- Post 3-5x per week for algorithm momentum

### TikTok
- Pickleball content growing fast, drill content has low competition
- Same short-form content as Reels (cross-post)
- Duet/stitch format: react to common mistakes, prescribe a drill

### Reddit (r/Pickleball — 130k+ members)
- Post the tool as "I built this free pickleball drill generator"
- Contribute genuine value in comments, link tool when relevant
- Don't spam — one well-placed post can drive sustained traffic

### YouTube Shorts
- Cross-post Reels/TikTok content
- Longer drill breakdowns (2-5 min) for YouTube proper — these rank in Google search

---

## Quick Wins (Do These First)

1. **Create 5-10 short "drill tip" graphics/videos** — each links back to the generator
2. **Post the tool in 3-5 Facebook pickleball groups** — "free tool I made for the community"
3. **Add a "Share your drill plan" button** to the tool (generates screenshot-friendly summary)
4. **Partner with local pickleball coaches** — coaches love free drill resources to share with students
5. **Create a Linktree or bio link page** pointing to all tools

## Funnel Summary

```
Social content (pain point hooks)
  -> Curiosity / "I need to practice that"
    -> Link in bio / direct link
      -> dinkoffame.com/tools/drill-generator
        -> Generate drill plan
          -> Bookmark / return visits
            -> Discover other tools, blog, app waitlist
```

The tool is the funnel bottom. Social content is the top. Every post should make someone think "I need to practice that" and give them an easy next step (the generator).
